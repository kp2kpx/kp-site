## The short version

Nobody assigned me the Spaces. I started hosting them because the builders around me needed a room, and a room is just a person willing to keep the mic on. That is the whole trick. You show up every week, you make it safe to demo something half broken, and over time the half broken things turn into shipped products.

## What a weekly cadence teaches you

A few things I did not expect:

- People do not need a polished stage. They need a recurring one. The reliability is the value.
- The best signal in an ecosystem is who keeps coming back, not who shows up once with a big announcement.
- Hosting is a skill. Pacing, drawing out the quiet builder, cutting off the rambler with grace. It compounds.

## Why this is on my own .eth

This site lives at kp2kp.eth, served from IPFS, updated with a single git push and zero gas after the first record is set. That is the same ethos as the Spaces: own your distribution, keep the cost near zero, and make showing up the only thing you have to remember to do.
